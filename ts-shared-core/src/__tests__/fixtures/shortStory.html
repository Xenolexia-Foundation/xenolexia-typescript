The house was old and the people there had a good life. One year she said to him, "I want to go out and see the world. We have time. When can we take the first step?" He would think about it and then make his way into the room. "This is the day," he said. "We will go from here. Just get your things and come with me." So they took some food and went on their way. They could not know what they would find. By the time the sun went down, they had come to a place where there were many people. "Who can help us?" she asked. "We need a place to stay. We have been on the road all day." One man looked at them and said, "I have a room. You can have it for the night. But first tell me about your life. Where do you come from? What do you do?" She said, "We come from the north. We want to see the world. This is our first year away from home." The man smiled. "Good. Come in. Take what you need. If you need more, just say so. My house is your house." So they went in and had some food. The next day they went on. They could see the hills and the trees. "I think we will find a new life here," she said. "I know it. We have come so far. When we go back, we will have so much to tell." He took her hand. "Yes. But for now we have each other. That is all we need." And so they walked on into the sun.

After that first night they grew more sure. Each day they would take the road before them and look for work. Some people gave them food. Others gave them a place to sleep. They did not have much but they had hope. One time a woman asked them, "Why do you go from place to place? Do you not want a home?" He said, "We want to see how other people live. We want to know the world before we find our own place." The woman nodded. "I understand. When you are old you will look back on this time. You will see that it was good." She was right. Many years later they would sit by the fire and tell their children about the year they went out into the world. They would say, "We had no plan. We just went. And what we found was more than we could have known."

So the two went on. They came to a town where the people spoke another way. At first they could not understand. But they learned. They learned how to ask for bread and water. They learned how to say thank you. They learned that a smile is the same in every place. One day they met a man who had been on the same road. He said, "I have been where you go. The way is long but the people are good. Take care of each other. That is all that matters." They took his words to heart. When the rain came they found a dry place. When the sun was hot they rested under a tree. They did not have much but they had each other. And in the end that was enough.

When the year was over they turned for home. They had seen new places and new faces. They had learned that the world is large and that people are kind. They went back to the old house. Nothing had changed and yet everything had changed. They had changed. So they sat down and wrote the story of their year. They wanted to remember every day. They wanted to tell their friends what they had seen. And they wanted to say to all who would hear: go out. Take the first step. The world is waiting.

If you have a dream, do not wait. The best time to start is now. Take one step and then another. You will find your way.

They never forgot that year. It became the story they told again and again. A story of two people who went out with nothing and came back with everything that mattered.
